Why Does Google Suspend Accounts Due to Unpaid Balances?

Google enforces strict payment policies to ensure advertisers comply with their financial obligations. A Google Ads unpaid balance suspension can occur due to the following reasons:

  1. Missed or Late Payments

    If your account has outstanding balances from previous ad campaigns that were not paid on time, Google may suspend your account until the amount is cleared.

  2. Payment Method Issues

    Google may suspend your account if there is an issue with your payment method, such as:

    • Expired credit card
    • Insufficient funds
    • Payment declined by your bank
    • Invalid billing details
  3. Policy Violations Related to Payments

    Repeated failures to settle dues or attempts to bypass Google’s payment system may trigger a policy violation, leading to a suspension.

  4. Linked Account Suspensions

    If another Google Ads account linked to yours has unpaid balances, your account may also face suspension as part of Google's enforcement actions.
